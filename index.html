Strategic Silence

Silencio Estratégico

An 8-chapter bilingual investigation into how Cuba was emptied — and why it matters to the world. Drawing on declassified US government archives, demographic data, economic analysis, and on-the-record statements by the actors themselves, Strategic Silence argues that the 67-year US–Cuba conflict was not a dispute. It was an arrangement.

"Cuba is not a country that is failing. It is a country that is working — just not for the people who live there. Because it is not a crisis. It is an arrangement. And arrangements, unlike crises, are not solved. They are maintained."

Published in English and Spanish. The flagship investigation of Bona Nova, with new chapters and updates as the 2026 transition develops in real time.

Bona Nova is an independent investigative journalism platform publishing structural analysis of US–Cuba policy, narrative influence, and humanitarian crisis — in English and Spanish. The platform covers breaking news and long-form investigations, and publishes the Intelligence Brief Cuba podcast.

Strategic Silence does not claim anyone lied. It claims something harder to refute: the truth was structured so that no one needed to. The exile believes his story. The revolutionary believes hers. The American politician believes he is defending freedom. The Cuban official believes he is resisting imperialism. Each is telling the truth as they received it — shaped by a system that needed them to believe exactly what they believe in order to keep functioning.

Bona Nova is written by Xenia Palma Fernández — an independent analyst and bilingual educator reporting from Havana, Mexico City, and the United States, with direct familial connection to Cuba's ongoing crisis and no affiliation with any government, think tank, foundation, or political organization.
